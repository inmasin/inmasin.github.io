Meet the Team

Pampa Ghosh, PhD

Founder Director

Pampa Ghosh is an accomplished Material Scientist and Metallurgist with over 20 years of experience in industrial research. She specializes in high-strength steels, material characterization, advanced analysis, phase transformation, and welding, and has made significant contributions to various industries through her expertise in product development and process innovation.
Pampa has led research teams at Tata Steel, where she developed and trained professionals, and has collaborated on cutting-edge projects at McGill University and Concordia University. With a strong track record of over 35 publications and 14 patents, she combines technical excellence with a passion for mentoring and knowledge sharing, making her a valuable contributor to both research and industry.

Ranjit K Ray, PhD

Chief Mentor

Professor Ranjit Kumar Ray is an AICTE-INAE Distinguished Visiting Professor at SRMIST Chennai and CMRIT Bengaluru. A former professor at IIT Kanpur, he also served as a distinguished visiting professor at the Dr. M. N. Dastur School of Materials Science and Engineering. Additionally, he spent nearly a decade as a Visiting Scientist and Consultant for the R&D division of Tata Steel.
Professor Ray holds dual PhDs, one from the University of Birmingham, UK, and the other from the University of Calcutta. He completed his M.E. and B.E. at the University of Calcutta.
With over 250 publications in international peer-reviewed journals and more than 7,500 citations, Professor Ray is a renowned figure in the field. His accolades include the Calcutta University Gold Medal, Kamani Gold Medal, G.D. Birla Gold Medal of the Indian Institute of Metals, Alexander von Humboldt Fellowship, and the Best Metallurgist's Award from the Government of India. He was elected Fellow of the Indian National Academy of Engineering in 1999 and the Indian Institute of Metals in 2001.
Professor Ray's career is distinguished by his significant contributions to materials science, particularly in the study of textures of materials, earning him international recognition.
